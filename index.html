Virus Force

Virus Force is twin-stick shooter where the player cannot directly destroy enemies.

You play as a B Cell, a type of white blood cell the produces antibodies, and your task is to mark as many viruses as possible for your buds, the "Killer" T Cells. As you play you have the ability to call the Killer T Cells whenever you like, but you are encouraged to mark as many viruses as you can before doing so as it greatly increases your score modifier. Different viruses have various behaviors that can make it difficult to have too many of them on the screen at once, thus creating a risk-reward playstyle. Made with Unreal Engine 4.

Frog Vs. Chef

Frog Vs. Chef is a small game prototype I made for a gamejam with two other participants.

The jam's theme was "frogs", so we decided to make a little game about a chef after some frogs. My partners created the art and music assets. I worked on programming the game and creating the gameplay. The game has three stages. First you collect frogs until you become part frog. At that point you continue eating more frogs with your new frog tongue. After a certain point the Big Momma Frog comes out and you have to duel her. Not every asset we wanted was created, but I was able to complete the game with a bit of programming art. This game was made in 3 days from scratch using Unreal Engine 4.
