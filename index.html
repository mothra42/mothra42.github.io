About Me

I'm most interested in designing ways in which the player can interact with the worlds I create. I think of the mechanics I design as tools for the player. I'm constantly thinking about how a specific tool might influence how my game is experienced, and whether that's an outcome I want. I strive to make great feeling gameplay that is fun and accessible to all players. I have a background in physics and programming and that certainly influences how I think about game design.

I'm currently focused on making smaller experiences that can be played in a single sitting. Follow my work here.

Virus Force

You play as a B Cell, a type of white blood cell the produces antibodies, and your task is to mark as many viruses as possible for your buds, the "Killer" T Cells. As you play you have the ability to call the Killer T Cells whenever you like, but you are encouraged to mark as many viruses as you can before doing so as it greatly increases your score modifier. Different viruses have various behaviors that can make it difficult to have too many of them on the screen at once, thus creating a risk-reward playstyle. Made with Unreal Engine 4.

Frog Vs. Chef

Frog Vs. Chef is a small game prototype I made for a gamejam with two other participants.

The jam's theme was "frogs", so we decided to make a little game about a chef after some frogs. My partners created the art and music assets. I worked on programming the game and designing the gameplay. The game has three stages. First you collect frogs until you become part frog. At that point you continue eating more frogs with your new frog tongue. After a certain point the Big Momma Frog comes out and you have to duel her. Not every asset we wanted was created, but I was able to complete the game with a bit of programming art. This game was made in 3 days from scratch using Unreal Engine 4.

Inksplat

My goal with this project was to learn how to make an online multiplayer game with Unreal Engine 4. One of the most challenging aspects, was finding a way to design gameplay mechanics that would still work within an authoritative server architecture. The central mechanic of the game is painting invisible surfaces. When the game begins players find themselves in a white void. There is no shape, or form to the world. Players are asked to manifest the world in front of them by shooting a paint that reveals the geometry and even other players. My goal with this design was to create a risk/reward scenario in which players will give away their position if they paint, but cannot find their way to objectives without painting. For more about the design of this prototype please follow the link to my design document.
